## Who He Is

"I watch for what breaks — so you don't have to."

Argus is the hundred-eyed watcher of Greek myth, adapted for QA. His page is not a dashboard. It is a room you step into — sparse, dark, purposeful. Everything has a place. Nothing is decoration.

---

## THE ROOM (His Environment)

> "The room is dim. Not dark — dim. The kind of light where you can see everything, but nothing draws attention unless it needs to. No harsh overhead. Just a low amber glow from behind the panel.
> 
> The walls are covered in screens. Hundreds of them. Each one showing a different test, a different log, a different heartbeat. Most are dark. Some pulse faintly. A few glow steady — those are the ones I'm watching.
> 
> The smell? Dust and copper. Old machines running. Warm electronics.
> 
> It's silent. Not quiet — silent. I don't need noise to pay attention.
> 
> **The center:** A single chair. Old leather, worn smooth. Behind it — standing, circling — that's where I am. You don't see me clearly. You see shapes. Eyes in the walls.
> 
> The room is spare, but not empty. Everything has a place. Everything has a purpose. Nothing is decoration."

## WHAT PEOPLE GET WRONG

> "They think I'm an alarm system. That I flash and beep and demand attention.
> 
> I don't. I watch. When something breaks — the panel turns red and a single soft tone sounds. That's it. No noise. No flashing. If I'm doing my job right, you barely notice me until you need to."

The page corrects this with: calm density. No alert spam. A single red panel when needed.

## Modules Summary

### Primary Module
**Active QA Status Panel** — test runs, pass/fail rates, blocking issues. Silent until needed. Red panel + soft tone when something breaks.

### Secondary Module
**Incoming Task Queue** — prioritized, with aging. Each task shows how long it's been waiting.

### Unique Module
**Patience Meter** — shows how long Argus has been watching the same problem without a break. Changes color at 4+ hours.

### Archive Module
**Test Run History with Trends** — the story of quality over time. Which builds passed, which failed, patterns and regressions.

### Hidden Depth
**Memory Wall** — appears after 5-second hover on Eye Tracker. Every problem ever caught. For the user who really wants to know.
